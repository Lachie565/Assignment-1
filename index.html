Project Idea

Mechanic’s Friend is a simple mobile application, designed to replace “Job Cards” frequently used in the automotive industry and increase transparency for customers. It’s a time and business management application, the main goal is to increase efficiency and give a simple solution to business owners with an easy-to-use alternative to paper job cards. It also allows the customer to view the exact changes carried out by technicians, through photos taken during their time spent on the vehicle. Through a smart phone, this application will allow employees on or off site to send a digital job card to their employer. This informs the employer of the labour, the relevant information of the vehicle or machine that was worked on, parts used and allowing for pictures to be attached. It also acts as a record for the customer to view the work that was carried out on their vehicle, something that isn’t currently available.

During my time working in the automotive industry in regional Victoria, it amazed me how few small businesses took advantage of the technology available. Inefficient time keeping methods and vague descriptions to the customer of work carried out frustrated me. Current automotive business management applications are complete overhauls of a business’s system. This includes invoicing, and stock databases. A simpler alternative, such as a mobile application would be a lot easier to integrate for small businesses’ whilst also increasing customer satisfaction. With 20.1 million registered vehicles as of 31 January 2021(https://www.abs.gov.au/statistics/industry/tourism-and-transport/motor-vehicle-census-australia/latest-release#key-statistics), there’s a huge market of individuals who could benefit from this.

Mechanic’s Friend is a a simple mobile phone application designed to be used on a day to do basis in the automotive industry. On login, an employee will set themselves up as an admin, allowing control of tasks, and access to all the users submitted tasks. Employees will have less privileges, they will only be able to submit and edit tasks. A calendar synchronised between members of the business will be used to organise the tasks delegated by the admin. This allows the employee to see their schedule from day to day and allow them to appropriately plan their work.

From these tasks, the employees can access the location of the job, the vehicle involved, and the time it is scheduled to be completed. This will allow an overview of operations for the week to increase efficiency of the business. Once a task is allocated to one of the employees, it will be visible on their calendar. From here, an employee can send information relevant to this task back to their employer. This information includes: - The time the job commences and is finished. - The vehicle identification number, make, model and year. - Images and comments of work carried out. - Parts used. - Any notable issues that were not in the scope of the job but are beneficial for the customer to be aware of. - Travel involved

This information is currently often included in a hard copy “job card”. By using Mechanics Friend, it will eliminate the need for work documentation to be carried around by employees and replaced by a smart phone. These job cards usually contain the information included above, but the inclusion of pictures is something not currently implemented in a lot of small businesses. Pictures are regularly taken by technicians to ensure a vehicle or part is returned to its original state, using the picture for reference. Including it into a job card gives the customer this insight also. After the work is completed by the employed technician, they can then upload their finished Job Card and his/her employer will be able to review the information instantly. This allows the employer to have access to completed works documentation. For completed jobs, an aesthetically pleasing overview of the task will be able to be printed. This can be shown to customers to allow them to see exactly when their vehicle was worked on, and what was done to it. This can be verified by pictures taken by technicians to increase transparency and help ensure customers are getting what they paid for. It also allows the customer to use these pictures to get a second opinion, in case of any believed misconduct by a business.

The only hardware required for use of this application is a smartphone with internet access. To create it, Either Java or Kotlin seems the best choice. Kotlin is more appealing to me as it’s a simpler language for beginners and easier to implement. There is also various “code free” options such as Appypie, Adalo and Composer Pro which could be used to create the application.

Simple programming knowledge will be required to implement this application. Although the nature of the application is quite simple, and I believe would be feasible for an individual or group of individuals with some hard work and determination. The hardware involved is very easy to access, with nearly everybody having a smart phone capable of running applications. If this project is successful, Mechanic’s Friend will be a useful resource to smaller automotive businesses. It’s an easy to implement alternative to paper, whilst also benefitting the customer by giving them a more in-depth look into the work they paid for. This could impact how the automotive industry communicates with its customers and create a more transparent environment for the consumer.
